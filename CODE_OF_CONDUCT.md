# Code of Conduct

## ü§ù Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## üåü Our Standards

### ‚úÖ Examples of Behavior That Contributes to a Positive Environment

- **Being respectful** - Showing empathy and kindness toward other people
- **Being constructive** - Providing helpful feedback and suggestions
- **Being collaborative** - Working together toward common goals
- **Being inclusive** - Welcoming people from all backgrounds
- **Being patient** - Understanding that people have different skill levels and learning paces
- **Being professional** - Maintaining professionalism in all interactions
- **Accepting responsibility** - Acknowledging mistakes and learning from them
- **Focusing on the community** - Prioritizing what's best for the overall community

**Positive Examples:**

```markdown
‚úÖ "Great work on this feature! Have you considered adding error handling 
   for edge cases? Here's an example: [code snippet]"

‚úÖ "I'm new to this codebase. Could someone point me to the right files 
   for implementing feature X?"

‚úÖ "I appreciate your feedback. You're right, my approach has some issues. 
   I'll refactor it using your suggestion."
```

### ‚ùå Examples of Unacceptable Behavior

- **Harassment** - Using sexualized language or imagery, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- **Trolling** - Posting inflammatory or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks
- **Discrimination** - Public or private harassment based on any protected characteristic
- **Privacy violations** - Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Unprofessional conduct** - Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting
- **Dismissive behavior** - Dismissing or attacking someone's opinions or contributions
- **Gatekeeping** - Telling someone they're not a "real" developer or similar exclusionary behavior

**Unacceptable Examples:**

```markdown
‚ùå "This code is terrible. Did you even try?"
‚ùå "Why don't you just Google it?"
‚ùå "This is basic stuff. You shouldn't be contributing if you don't know this."
‚ùå Using derogatory language or personal attacks
```

## üë• Our Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

### What Maintainers Will Do

- **Clarify standards** - Explain expectations for behavior
- **Enforce standards** - Take action when standards are violated
- **Be fair** - Apply standards consistently and fairly
- **Protect the community** - Remove, edit, or reject contributions that violate this Code of Conduct
- **Communicate decisions** - Explain enforcement decisions when appropriate

## üåç Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, including:

- **GitHub repository** - Issues, pull requests, discussions, code reviews
- **Communication channels** - Email, Discord, Slack, or other official channels (if created)
- **Social media** - When officially representing the project
- **Public events** - Meetups, conferences, or other gatherings where you represent the project

It also applies when representing the project in public spaces, such as using an official email address, posting via an official social media account, or acting as an appointed representative.

## üìã Enforcement

### Reporting Issues

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to the community leaders responsible for enforcement at:

üìß **[conduct@sanitasmind.app](mailto:conduct@sanitasmind.app)**

All complaints will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly.

### What to Include in a Report

When reporting an issue, please provide:

1. **Your contact information** - So we can follow up with you
2. **Description of the incident** - What happened?
3. **When and where it occurred** - Link to the issue/PR/discussion
4. **Who was involved** - GitHub usernames of people involved
5. **Context** - Any additional context that might be helpful
6. **Desired outcome** - What would you like to see happen?

**Confidentiality:** All community leaders are obligated to respect the privacy and security of the reporter of any incident.

### Example Report

```markdown
Date: February 15, 2026
Reporter: @username
Location: GitHub Issue #123

Description: User @other-user made dismissive comments about my code 
contribution, suggesting I "learn to code" before contributing. This 
happened after I submitted my first pull request.

Context: This is my first contribution to open source, and while I'm 
learning, the comment was discouraging and violated the Code of Conduct.

Desired Outcome: I'd like the comment to be removed and for the user 
to understand why their behavior was inappropriate.
```

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. üìù Correction

**Community Impact:** Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence:** A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

**Example:** Making an insensitive joke in a pull request comment.

### 2. ‚ö†Ô∏è Warning

**Community Impact:** A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence:** A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

**Example:** Repeatedly dismissing others' contributions or making unwelcoming comments.

### 3. ‚è∏Ô∏è Temporary Ban

**Community Impact:** A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence:** A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

**Example:** Harassment of community members or aggressive behavior toward maintainers.

### 4. üö´ Permanent Ban

**Community Impact:** Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence:** A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

**Example:** Severe harassment, doxxing, or repeated violations after warnings.

## üéØ Scope and Application

### For Contributors

- Be respectful in code reviews
- Provide constructive feedback
- Help newcomers learn and grow
- Focus on the code, not the person
- Accept feedback gracefully

### For Maintainers

- Lead by example

### For Users

- Report bugs respectfully
- Provide detailed, helpful information
- Be patient when waiting for responses
- Thank contributors for their work
- Follow community guidelines

## üí¨ Positive Communication Guidelines

### Giving Feedback

**Before commenting, ask yourself:**
- Is this necessary?
- Is this kind?
- Is this constructive?

**When giving feedback:**
- Start with something positive
- Be specific about what needs improvement
- Suggest solutions, not just problems
- Use "I" statements ("I think" instead of "You should")
- Offer to help if you can

**Good feedback examples:**

```markdown
‚úÖ "Nice work on this feature! I noticed that error handling could be 
   improved in the uploadReceipt function. Would you like me to suggest 
   some examples?"

‚úÖ "I really like the approach you took here. One thing to consider: 
   what happens if the API returns an unexpected response? Maybe we 
   could add validation for that case."

‚úÖ "Thanks for submitting this PR! The logic looks solid. I left some 
   inline comments with suggestions for improving readability."
```

### Receiving Feedback

- Assume good intentions
- Don't take criticism personally
- Ask clarifying questions if needed
- Thank people for their time
- It's okay to disagree respectfully

## üåà Diversity and Inclusion

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inclusive environment for all contributors, regardless of:

- **Experience level** - From first-time contributors to seasoned developers
- **Background** - All cultural, ethnic, and national backgrounds
- **Identity** - All gender identities and expressions
- **Ability** - All levels of physical and cognitive ability
- **Technology choices** - Different tools, languages, and approaches

### Inclusive Language

- Use gender-neutral language (they/them as default)
- Avoid assumptions about someone's background or experience
- Use inclusive terminology (avoid terms with problematic histories)
- Be mindful of cultural differences
- Ask for people's pronouns if relevant
